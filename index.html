The Franklin Expedition: A Legacy of Resilience

Historical Overview

In 1845, Sir John Franklin led an expedition to navigate the Northwest Passage. HMS Erebus and HMS Terror became icebound in 1846 near King William Island, leading to the loss of all 129 crew after Franklin’s death in 1847.

Crew Resilience

The crew’s determination shone through as they trekked south, enduring starvation and resorting to cannibalism, evidenced by bone cut marks, in a bid to survive the Arctic’s harshness.

Archaeological Discoveries

HMS Erebus was discovered in 2014, and HMS Terror in 2016, yielding artifacts like the Erebus bell and tools, with DNA identifying crew like James Fitzjames.

Current Excavations

Parks Canada, with Inuit guidance, uses advanced technology to explore the wrecks, recovering artifacts and identifying remains, preserving this historic site.
